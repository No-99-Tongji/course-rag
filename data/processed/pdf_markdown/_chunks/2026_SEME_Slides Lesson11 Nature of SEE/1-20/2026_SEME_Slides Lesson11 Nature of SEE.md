## 11.1 Introduction

## What do we learn from this case?

This case study illustrates the variety of issues with which managerial economics is concerned.

The following questions arise:

1. Why are software/information service companies developing rapidly?

2. Are there solutions to the problem, in terms of strategy/action that can be taken?

3. What constraints or conditions exist in terms of operating any strategies?

4. How do we evaluate these strategies in terms of costs and benefits?

5. What are the effective ways of measuring the relevant variables of costs?

6. What assumptions should be made in our analysis of software engineering?

7. How do we deal with the problem of risk and uncertainty regarding to the effects of strategies of software project/product in the future?

8. What criteria can we use for selecting the better strategies and taking action?

Software engineering economics will provide you the powerful methods/tools to solve above questions.

## 11.2 Definition

## Definition of Software Engineering Economics (SEE)

## Software Engineering Economics

The application of economic theory and methods to software engineering business decision-making. As such it can be seen as a means to an end by managers, in terms of finding the most efficient way of allocating their scarce resources, optimizing business model & design, verifying and achieving their goals.

## Business (业务)

The term ‘business’ must be defined very broadly in this course. It applies to any situation where there is a transaction between two or more parties/stakeholders.

## Related theoretical foundations

The software engineering economics involves the following theories:

1. Theory and Practices of Software Engineering (base)

2. Economic Theory (base)

3. Decision Science (tools)

4. Business and Accounting theory (context and metrics)

5. Project Management (process)

## 11.2 Definition

## Economic Theory

## Assumption

This is essentially an approach that treats the individual elements within the economy (consumers, firms and workers) as rational agents with objectives that can be expressed as quantitative functions (utilities and profits) that are to be optimized, subject to certain quantitative constraints.

Essentially Positive statements are factual statements whose truth or falsehood can be verified by empirical study or logic. Normative statements involve a value judgement and cannot be verified by empirical study or logic.

Example: Positive vs Normative statements

1. The distribution of income in China is unequal (不公平).

2. The distribution of income in China is inequitable (不公正).

The first statement is a positive one while the second is a normative one. Normative statements often imply a recommendation, in the above example that income should be redistributed. For that reason they often involve the words ought or should.

The decision sciences provide the tools and techniques of analysis used in decision making.

All of these functional areas can apply the theories and methods mentioned earlier, in the context of the particular situation and tasks that they have to perform.

## Allocation of resources

In most situations, you are not the only man in software project. Other people’s behavior will influence what you decide to do. Economists think of the world as a large number of economic agents who are interacting and influencing one another’s efforts at optimization.

## 11.6 Principles of SEE

## Optimization

Optimization is the most important principle of SEE.

Software engineers believe that optimization — trying to pick the best feasible option — explains the choices that people make, including minor decisions like accepting an invitation to see a movie and major decisions like deciding whom to marry. Decisions aren’t made with a crystal ball. People try to do as well as they can, given the limited information, knowledge, experience, and training that they have.

For example, Algorithm optimization refers to the optimization of the relevant performance of the algorithm, such as time complexity, space complexity, correctness and robustness. Because the application scenario of the algorithm changes greatly, the algorithm optimization can make the algorithm have the ability to better meet the needs of users.

Case study Unbundling of software and hardware (IBM, 1969)

In 1969, IBM announced the unbundling of system/360 application software and mainframe hardware. IBM System/360 mainframe, which was introduced by IBM in 1964, is a general-purpose computer system. Its business goal is that users can continuously upgrade the host hardware according to the growing demand of their business transaction ability without replacing the host application program. This provides the technical feasibility of separating software from hardware.

In order to resist the competition of RCA, IBM decided to decouple the host application software from the mainframe. IBM announced that its software and hardware are unbound. This has brought the birth of the software industry.

## 11.6 Principles of SEE

## Optimization: Trade-offs and Budget Constraints

Economists use budget constraints to describe trade-offs.

A budget constraint (预算约束) is the set of things that a person can choose to do (or buy) without breaking her budget.

Suppose: Only one of two activities with each hour of your free time:

— use social media.

— work at a part-time job.

Suppose that you have 5 free hours in a day (once we take away necessities like sleeping, eating, bathing, attending classes, doing problem sets, and studying for exams). Think of these 5 free hours as your budget of free time. Then your budget constraint would be:

## 5 hours = Hours on social media + Hours working at part-time job

This equation, which is also called a budget constraint, implies that you face a trade-off.

## 11.6 Principles of SEE

## Optimization: Cost-Benefit Analysis

Cost-benefit analysis is a calculation that identifies the best option by summing benefits and subtracting costs, with both benefits and costs denominated in a common unit of measurement, like Yuan(RMB).

Cost-benefit analysis is used to identify the alternative that has the greatest net benefit, which is the sum of the benefits of choosing an alternative minus the sum of the costs of choosing that alternative.

Example: Trip planning from Shanghai to Beijing

— By Car driving, by train or by air ?

To analyze this problem using cost-benefit analysis, you need to list all benefits and costs of driving compared to the alternative of flying. Here we’ll express these benefits and costs comparatively, which means the benefits of driving compared to flying and the costs of driving compared to flying. We’ll need to translate those benefits and costs into a common unit of measurement.

## 11.6 Principles of SEE

## Verification & Validation

Software development, like other industries, needs the continuous input of production factors before there is output. In addition to considering the balance of supply and demand(Equilibrium), how to distribute output among market participants is also an issue that needs to be considered in economics.

Economists test their ideas with data. We refer to such evidence-based analysis as empirical analysis or empiricism. Economists use data to determine whether our theories about human behavior — like optimization, equilibrium and allocation — match up with actual human behavior.

Of course, we want to know if our theories fail to explain what is happening in the world. In that case, we need to go back to the practice and come up with better theories. That is how software engineering economics progresses in general.

But there are some cases when cause and effect are hard to untangle. Does being relatively smart cause people to go to college, or does going to college cause people to be relatively smart? Perhaps both directions of causation apply.
